## Interview 1 — R.K., CTO, 8-person SaaS startup, Seed stage

**Date:** 2026-05-07 | **Duration:** 12 minutes | **Method:** Twitter DM → Google Meet

**Background:** Running a B2B analytics product. Team uses Cursor, Claude, and ChatGPT simultaneously. Monthly AI bill around $400.

**Direct quotes:**
- "I literally don't know what half my team is using. I just pay the invoices."
- "We have both Cursor and Copilot active. I know that's probably stupid but switching takes time."
- "If something showed me the exact number I'm wasting, I'd act on it same day."

**Most surprising thing:** He didn't know Claude Team required a minimum of 5 seats. He had 3 people on it. That's $90/month wasted — a rule already in the audit engine.

**What it changed:** Added a "you're on a plan with a minimum seat requirement you're not meeting" rule explicitly. Made the reason text very specific about the minimum seat count.

## Interview 2 — S.M., Indie Hacker, solo founder, pre-revenue

**Date:** 2026-05-08 | **Duration:** 10 minutes | **Method:** Indie Hackers DM

**Background:** Building a solo SaaS product. Uses ChatGPT Plus and Claude Pro. Concerned about costs scaling as she adds team members.

**Direct quotes:**
- "I always assumed the more expensive plan was better. Nobody told me otherwise."
- "I want to know when I should upgrade, not just when I'm overspending."
- "A shareable link is actually really useful — I'd send this to my co-founder before we discuss tools."

**Most surprising thing:** She was already on the optimal plan for her situation but wanted a "you're good" confirmation, not just savings advice. She said "tell me I'm spending well" was as valuable as finding savings.

**What it changed:** Added the "You're spending well" state in AuditHero explicitly with its own positive messaging rather than just showing $0 savings with no context.

## Interview 3 — A.P., Engineering Manager, 15-person startup, Series A

**Date:** 2026-05-09 | **Duration:** 15 minutes | **Method:** College network WhatsApp

**Background:** Manages a team of 10 engineers. Budget owner for all dev tooling. AI bill around $1,200/month across multiple tools.

**Direct quotes:**
- "My CFO asked me last month to justify the AI spend. I had no good answer."
- "I need something I can screenshot and put in a Slack message to my CEO."
- "The Credex thing — I'd actually click that if the savings number was real and big."

**Most surprising thing:** The shareable URL feature was the most compelling thing to him — not the audit itself, but having a professional-looking report he could forward internally to justify or cut budget.

**What it changed:** Prioritized the visual quality of the results page significantly. Made the hero savings number larger and cleaner. Added the OG image meta tags so the shared link previews well in Slack.
