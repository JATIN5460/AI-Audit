# GTM.md

## Exact target user

Engineering Manager or CTO at a seed to Series A startup, 5-20 person team, using 3+ AI tools simultaneously. They approved each tool subscription individually over 6 months and have never looked at the combined bill. They feel AI tools are "necessary" but have a vague sense they're paying for overlap. Job title: Head of Engineering, CTO, VP Engineering. Company stage: post-product-market-fit, pre-Series B.

## What they Google before wanting this tool

- "cursor vs github copilot which is better"
- "reduce saas spend startup"
- "ai tools for developers cost comparison"
- "chatgpt team vs claude team"
- "cutting costs series a startup"

## Where they hang out online

- r/ExperiencedDevs and r/cscareerquestions for tool discussions
- Hacker News — Show HN posts on dev tools get traction
- Indie Hackers community (many solo founders and small teams)
- X/Twitter — following @levelsio, @marc_louvion, @swyx type accounts
- Software engineering Slack groups — Rands Leadership Slack, Software Lead Weekly

## First 100 users in 30 days with $0

**Week 1:** Post on Hacker News as Show HN: "I built a free tool that audits your AI tool spend." HN has historically responded well to free, no-login tools that provide instant value. Target 200+ upvotes.

**Week 2:** Cold DM 50 CTOs/EMs on X who have tweeted about AI tools or SaaS costs. Message: "Built a free AI spend auditor — took me 2 min to find $400/mo I was wasting. Here's the link." Attach screenshot of a real audit result.

**Week 3:** Post in Indie Hackers "What tools are you using" threads with the tool linked. Post in r/ExperiencedDevs as a discussion about AI tool overlap — link the tool as context.

**Week 4:** Reach out to 5 developer-focused newsletters (TLDR, Software Lead Weekly, Pointer) offering a free audit of their own stack in exchange for a mention.
